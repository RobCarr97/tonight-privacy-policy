Child Sexual Abuse and Exploitation (CSAE) Standards

Tonight - Social Dating App

Effective Date: October 2, 2025
Zero Tolerance Policy: Tonight has a strict zero-tolerance policy against child sexual abuse and exploitation of any kind.

1. Age Verification and Protection

Minimum Age Requirements

  • All users must be 18 years of age or older
  • Age verification is required during registration
  • Users suspected of being under 18 are immediately suspended
  • Government-issued ID verification may be required

Age Verification Process

  • Date of birth collection during signup
  • Cross-reference with provided identification
  • Automated systems to detect potential age misrepresentation
  • Manual review of suspicious accounts

2. Content Monitoring and Detection

Automated Systems

  • AI-powered content scanning for inappropriate material
  • PhotoDNA technology integration for CSAM detection
  • Behavioral analysis to identify suspicious patterns
  • Real-time monitoring of messages and media

Human Review

  • Trained content moderators review flagged content
  • 24/7 monitoring capabilities
  • Regular audits of detection systems
  • Continuous training on emerging threats

3. Reporting and Response

User Reporting

  • Easy-to-access reporting buttons throughout the app
  • Anonymous reporting options available
  • Multiple reporting categories including CSAE
  • Priority handling of child safety reports

Response Procedures

  • Immediate suspension of accounts involved in CSAE
  • Preservation of evidence for law enforcement
  • Removal of all associated content
  • Permanent ban with device and IP tracking

4. Law Enforcement Cooperation

  • Immediate reporting to National Center for Missing & Exploited Children (NCMEC)
  • Full cooperation with law enforcement investigations
  • Preservation of digital evidence as required by law
  • Regular communication with child safety organizations

5. Prevention Measures

Technical Safeguards

  • Hash-based detection of known CSAM
  • Machine learning algorithms for pattern recognition
  • Regular security audits and penetration testing
  • Encrypted data transmission and storage

User Education

  • Clear community guidelines regarding appropriate content
  • Regular safety reminders and notifications
  • Resources for reporting suspicious behavior
  • Partnership with child safety organizations

6. Staff Training and Protocols

  • Mandatory CSAE awareness training for all employees
  • Regular updates on detection techniques
  • Clear escalation procedures for suspected cases
  • Psychological support for staff reviewing disturbing content

7. Regular Audits and Updates

  • Quarterly review of safety policies and procedures
  • Annual third-party security assessments
  • Continuous improvement based on emerging threats
  • Regular consultation with child safety experts

8. Transparency Reporting

  • Annual transparency reports on content moderation
  • Statistics on CSAE detection and removal
  • Collaboration with industry safety initiatives
  • Public accountability for child safety measures
